The Boy Who Became Cricket's Immortal

Born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar was destined for cricketing immortality. His prodigious talent was evident from childhood when he scored an unbeaten 326 runs in a school match at just 11 years old.

At the tender age of 16, Sachin made his international debut against Pakistan, facing one of cricket's most fearsome bowling attacks. His technical perfection, combined with an unflappable temperament under pressure, quickly marked him as a once-in-a-generation talent.

Over his 24-year career, Sachin became cricket's most complete batsman, combining classical technique with innovative strokeplay. His straight drive became the gold standard of batting, executed with a grace and precision that left spectators and opponents in awe.

Records That Redefined Cricket

100 International Centuries

The only player in history to score 100 international centuries across Tests and ODIs - a milestone that may never be surpassed.

First ODI Double Century

Redefined limited-overs batting by becoming the first player to score a double century in ODI cricket against South Africa.

Bharat Ratna

Honored with India's highest civilian award, becoming the first sportsperson and youngest recipient of the Bharat Ratna.

Global Recognition

Named in Time magazine's 100 most influential people in the world and awarded honorary membership to the Order of Australia.

World Cup Champion

Played a pivotal role in India's 2011 World Cup victory, fulfilling the dream of a billion people after six tournament appearances.

Wisden Cricketer

Named Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World twice and featured in Wisden's All-Time Test World XI.
